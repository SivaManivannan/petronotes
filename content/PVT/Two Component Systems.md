## Two phase p-T

Figure below is a p-T projection of ethane/n-heptane system for a fixed composition. Multi component systems for most purposes is a simple extension of the two component system.

![[p-T two component system.png]]

Two main differences exist when compared to [[Single Component Systems]]

1. For single component system, the dew point and bubble point curves are one and the same which is the vapor-pressure curve. For multi-component systems, The saturated p-T projection is represented by a phase envelope rather than by a vapor pressure curve. As a result pressure and temperature affect phase behavior in the saturated region. This is a natural consequence of [[Gibbs Phase rule]] $(F=2)$.

2. The [[Critical Parameters|Critical Pressure]] and [[Critical Parameters|Critical Temperature]] no longer define the extent of the two phase vapor/liquid region. These are instead pressure and temperature at which the dew and bubble point curves intersect at which the two phases are no longer distinguishable ([[Critical Parameters|Critical Point]]).

The highest temperature at which two phases can co-exist in equilibrium is defined as the cricondentherm. Similarly, the highest pressure at which two phases can co-exist is cricondenbar (tangent a-a).

### p-T curves for varying compositions

Figure below gives the phase behavior of binary ethane/n-heptane system at different compositions.

![[p-T two component varying composition.png]]

On the very left and right are the vapor-pressure curves of pure ethane and n-heptane, respectively.

Several interesting features of binary/multicomponent system can be studied from the figure above.

- Critical pressures of many mixtures (but not all) are higher than the critical pressures of pure components
- With a mixture composed mainly of ethane the critical point lies to the left of the cricondentherm. Such system are analogous to [[Classification of Oil Field Systems|Gas condensate]] systems.
- The critical point for the mixture composed mostly of n-heptane lies below the cricondenbar. This system is analogous to a reservoir [[Classification of Oil Field Systems|Black oil]] system
